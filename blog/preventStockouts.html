Prevent stockouts with smart alerts

Flip keeps you ahead of demand by flagging risk early—so you reorder on time, not in panic.

How Flip decides when to alert

Alerts use a simple, reliable picture of your real availability.

  • On‑hand: what you physically have now.
  • Committed: quantities already promised on open sales/orders.
  • Available: on‑hand − committed.
  • Minimum level: the floor you set per item (often lead‑time demand + safety stock).

Flip raises a Warning when available ≈ minimum, and a Critical alert when available < minimum. That means you act before customers feel the impact.

Set smart minimums (quick recipe)

  1. Lead‑time demand: average daily sales × supplier lead time (days).
  2. Safety stock: 1–2 weeks of sales, or use your demand variability.
  3. Minimum = lead‑time demand + safety stock.

Example: sell 5/day, 10‑day lead time, 1‑week safety stock (35 units) → minimum = 85.

What an alert tells you (and what to do)

  • Warning: order soon. Create a purchase order sized to cover lead‑time demand + buffer.
  • Critical: act now. Transfer from another location, expedite a vendor order, or adjust counts if a discrepancy caused the drop.

Workflow that prevents surprises

  1. Review today’s alerts each morning (takes minutes).
  2. For each alert, choose: Purchase, Transfer, or Adjust.
  3. After receiving or transferring, Flip updates availability automatically.

Real‑world examples

  • Seasonal spikes: raise minimums for SKUs with upcoming promos; lower afterward to avoid overstock.
  • Multi‑location: a store is critical, warehouse has surplus—transfer beats rush shipping.
  • Bundles/kits: watch components; a single missing trim can block finished goods.

Get started in five minutes

  1. Pick top 10 sellers and add minimums using the recipe above.
  2. Turn on alerts for email or in‑app notifications.
  3. Create a simple weekly review: adjust minimums as sales shift.

Result: fewer fire drills, steadier cash flow, and happier customers.
